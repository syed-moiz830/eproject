Grunge

Grunge rock is a raw, gritty subgenre of rock music that emerged in the late 1980s and early 1990s, especially from Seattle’s indie music scene, blending punk’s raw energy with heavy rock’s power. It’s characterized by heavily distorted electric guitars, thick basslines, and strong yet simple drum rhythms that create a rugged, unpolished sound. Grunge songs often shift between quiet verses and loud, explosive choruses, expressing emotional tension and contrast. Vocals range from soulful and melodic to raw and anguished, matching lyrics that explore alienation, frustration, introspection, and social issues. Unlike more polished rock genres, grunge embraces an authentic, DIY aesthetic, often shunning technical showmanship for heartfelt expression. The genre drew influence from punk rock’s attitude and heavy metal’s dense guitar tones, giving it a distinctive edge. Bands like Nirvana, Pearl Jam, Soundgarden, and Alice in Chains helped embody this sound.
