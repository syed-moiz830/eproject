Nu-Jazz

Traditional jazz, often called Dixieland or New Orleans jazz, emerged in the early 20th century as one of the first styles of jazz music. It originated in New Orleans, blending African rhythms, blues, ragtime, and European brass band traditions into a lively and spontaneous sound. Musicians improvised together in collective ensembles, meaning multiple instruments would build around a shared melody while adding personal flourishes. A typical traditional jazz band includes horns like trumpet, clarinet, and trombone, supported by rhythm instruments such as piano, drums, and bass. This style emphasizes swing, syncopation, and joyful interaction between players. Traditional jazz paved the way for later jazz forms by celebrating solo expression and rhythmic freedom
