About Artist

Muzio Clementi (1752–1832), often revered as the "Father of the Pianoforte," was a towering Italian-born British composer, pianist, pedagogue, and innovative businessman whose career bridged the late Classical and early Romantic eras. Born in Rome, he was a child prodigy who secured an organist position at age 13 and composed an oratorio by 14. His immense talent attracted the attention of Englishman Peter Beckford, who sponsored him to study in Dorset, England, for seven years, where Clementi developed a formidable, virtuosic technique. Following a move to London and a successful European tour—notably featuring a famous, high-stakes duel with Mozart in 1781—Clementi established himself as a premier performer and composer.
